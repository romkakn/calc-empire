This doc is the source of truth for blog-post topics. Posts are written to:

---

## 1. Methodology

Every candidate is scored on four axes:

| Axis | What it means | Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| **Volume** | Avg monthly US searches (Ahrefs) | ≥ 500/mo to consider |
| **KD** | Keyword Difficulty 0–100 (Ahrefs) | ≤ 30 ideal; ≤ 45 acceptable |
| **Intent** | Informational vs commercial vs transactional | Must be informational |
| **Anchor calc** | Which live calculator the post funnels into | Required — no orphan posts |

A keyword is **WINNABLE** if KD ≤ 30 AND (volume ≥ 1,000 OR commercial intent + CPC ≥ $2).
A keyword is **STRETCH** if KD 31–45 with vol ≥ 3,000.
A keyword is **HARD-PASS** if KD ≥ 60 unless the SERP is dominated by stale Khan-Academy-style results that we can outpunch on UX.

## 5. Editorial template (every post)

Every blog post follows the structure proven on `/blog/how-much-concrete-do-i-need`:

1. **Header** — title, eyebrow (category · reading time · published date), tagline
2. **CTA Card** — links the anchor calculator (skip-the-math option)
3. **The one formula / the key concept** — single concise section
4. **Step-by-step** — numbered list, plain English
5. **Three worked examples** — covers the edge cases SERP competitors miss
6. **Why the [thing] isn't optional** — opinionated section, this is where voice goes
7. **Decision tree section** — "X vs Y, picking your method"
8. **CommonMistakes block** — reused E-E-A-T component
9. **FAQ block** — 6–10 PAA-aligned Q&A, fed into FAQPage JSON-LD
10. **References block** — 3–5 authoritative sources, real titles
11. **Author + LastReviewed**
12. **RelatedCalculators** — 3 cross-links

Required SEO tech:
- `<title>` ≤ 60 chars (the post-1 bracketed sub-phrase is the intentional exception)
- `<meta description>` ≤ 155 chars
- canonical `/blog/<slug>` exactly
- OG: type=article, publishedTime, modifiedTime
- JSON-LD: Article + FAQPage + BreadcrumbList + Person
- Robots: index, follow (every post; only `/guide` was noindex)

Required humanization (per `/humanizer` skill):
- Zero em dashes (U+2014) — repeat: `grep -c '—' src/app/blog/<slug>/page.tsx` must return 0
- Zero AI vocabulary blacklist (`delve`, `vibrant`, `robust`, `leverage`, `pivotal`, `crucial`, `underscore`, `foster`, `tapestry`, `landscape`, `realm`, `notably`, `additionally`, `moreover`, `furthermore`)
- High burstiness: sentence lengths range 5–40 words within each paragraph
- One opinionated section per post ("Why X isn't optional" or similar)

## 7. Lessons from post #1 (concrete)

- **Worked-example length matters more than total word count.** The concrete post's 3 examples cover the patio/garage/footing range and that's where it'll win the long-tail.
- **Em-dash-replacement broke spacing.** When you replace " — " with "." you lose the trailing space; we re-fixed it via a Python script. For the next post, write the prose with periods/colons from the start.
- **The CTA card belongs after the lead, not at the bottom.** Readers who came for "tell me the number" need to get to the calc immediately; the math is for readers who stayed.
- **Cross-link 3 calcs per post, not more.** More dilutes the funnel. Less feels orphaned.
- **References block beats inline footnotes for SEO.** Bot-readable, schema-friendly, doesn't break prose flow.
